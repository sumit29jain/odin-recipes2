Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian savory crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Crispy on the edges and soft in the middle, dosa is typically served with coconut chutney and sambar, a flavorful lentil-based vegetable stew.

One of the most appealing aspects of dosa is its adaptability. You can customize the filling according to your preferences, whether it's a classic potato masala, paneer, cheese, or even Nutella for a sweet variation. Dosa is not only delicious but also nutritious, as it is naturally gluten-free and low in calories.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water (as needed for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  1. Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak them in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the water and grind the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together to make a smooth batter. Add water as needed to achieve a pouring consistency.
  3. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it outwards in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  5. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the bottom is golden brown and crisp.
  6. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute until the other side is cooked through.
  7. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
